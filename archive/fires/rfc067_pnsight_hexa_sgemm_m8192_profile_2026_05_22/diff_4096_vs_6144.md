# Diff: M=4096 (peak, ratio 0.819) vs M=6144 (cliff, ratio 0.234)

ncu kernel-replay metrics on RTX 5070 (sm_120), N107 4-warp 64x64 hexa SGEMM PTX,
mean of 4 replay invocations per shape.

## Key observation

**M=4096 -> M=6144 is the cliff edge.** L2 hit rate drops from 98% to 57% in one step.
Working set goes from 64MB (2x L2) to 144MB (4.5x L2). The L2's effective reuse for
tile-blocked GEMM collapses.

When L2 misses go up:
- Memory latency per warp instruction increases (41 -> 122 cycles)
- Eligible warps drop to 0.10 (out of 7.95 active per scheduler)
- DRAM traffic explodes (127 MB -> 6470 MB = 50x for 3.4x more work)
- Compute SM throughput drops 40% -> 14% (compute pipeline starved)

This is the textbook signature of **L2 capacity miss with no compensating prefetch**.

## What's NOT happening

- Occupancy is NOT the issue (66% both shapes, theoretical limit is register pressure)
- L1/TEX hit rate is NOT the cliff (it's already ~zero at M=4096; ldmatrix bypasses L1)
- SMs ARE active 99.9% of cycles (smsp__cycles_active.pct = 99.86% at M=6144)
- The kernel is not idle -- it's *issuing* warps; the warps are *stalled on memory*
