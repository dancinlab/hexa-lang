## Key observation

**M=8192 is just a deeper L2 thrash, not a new mechanism.** Compared to M=6144:

| Metric | M=6144 | M=8192 | Delta |
|--------|-------:|-------:|------:|
| L2 hit rate | 56.72% | 50.44% | -6.28 pp |
| Warp cycles/inst | 121.94 | 141.53 | +16% |
| DRAM throughput | 33.83% | 33.34% | -0.49 pp (saturated) |
| Compute SM | 14.00% | 12.16% | -1.84 pp |

DRAM bandwidth saturates at ~224 GB/s at both M=6144 and M=8192 (RTX 5070 peak DRAM
is approx 650-700 GB/s but ncu reports `pct_of_peak_sustained_elapsed` ~34% which
maps to that GB/s value). Once DRAM is saturated, more work just means longer wait.

**Confirms**: cliff is purely L2 capacity (capacity miss). L1, occupancy, scheduler
register pressure, and compute pipeline are all unchanged. The structural ratio
recovery at M=8192 vs M=6144 (ratio 0.304 > 0.234) is because cuBLAS itself slows
down at M=8192 (70.8 -> 45.7 TFLOPS, see N130 result.json) -- our denominator
shrank, not because hexa got faster.

## ratio_vs_cublas non-monotonicity explained

N130 result.json:
- M=4096: hexa 57.3, cuBLAS 70.0, ratio 0.819
- M=6144: hexa 16.6, cuBLAS 70.8, ratio 0.234
- M=8192: hexa 13.9, cuBLAS 45.7, ratio 0.304

Hexa decays monotonically (57.3 -> 16.6 -> 13.9 TFLOPS); cuBLAS dips at M=8192 (45.7),
making the ratio appear to recover. The actual structural cliff is monotone in
hexa absolute performance.
