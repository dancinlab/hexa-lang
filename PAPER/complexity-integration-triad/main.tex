\begin{abstract}
A recurring claim across physics, category theory, and network science is
that \emph{more structured} systems are \emph{more integrated} or have
\emph{rougher} energy landscapes. Such claims are easy to confound with raw
edge or bond density: a denser system is trivially both more structured and
more integrated. We ask the sharper question --- does a structural invariant
predict a landscape property \emph{after density is held fixed}? --- on three
independent substrates: a $3{\times}3$ Edwards--Anderson $\pm J$ Ising lattice,
an $N{=}6$ directed category-graph, and an $N{=}6$ undirected graph. Each
substrate is measured by exact enumeration (no sampling, no learning, integer
or rational measures only), under a 5-falsifier protocol frozen before any run
(post-hoc tuning count $=0$). The headline is the density-controlled falsifier
F3, which holds on all three substrates: with edge probability pinned at
$p{=}0.4$, the high-structure half is more integrated / rougher than the
low-structure half (integration $\Phi=0.958$ vs $0.775$ for composition-closure;
$0.867$ vs $0.617$ for cycle topology; ruggedness $R$ scales $1.6\times$ with
frustration). Two pre-registered sub-falsifiers fail and are kept as findings:
an unfrustrated ferromagnet already has $R{=}32$ single-flip minima (frustration
\emph{amplifies}, it does not \emph{create} ruggedness), and random morphism
graphs do not self-organize into composition-closed categories even at
$p{=}0.6$ ($\bar D = 0.576 < 0.85$; closure needs designed structure, not
density). All numbers are reproducible bit-for-bit from the deterministic
harnesses; every verdict is the verbatim \texttt{hexa} run output recorded in
\texttt{companion/verify-ledger.json}. We claim only the closed-form proxies
stated; full IIT-$\Phi$ and persistent homology are out of scope.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

``Complexity begets integration'' is a folk theorem that appears, in different
vocabularies, in spin-glass physics (frustration roughens the energy
landscape~\citep{toulouse1977,binderyoung1986}), in the theory of consciousness
(integrated information rises with causal structure~\citep{tononi2004,oizumi2014}),
in category theory (composition closure is what makes a graph a genuine
category~\citep{maclane1971}), and in network science (cyclic, high-genus
topologies carry more information than trees~\citep{bianconi2021}). The trouble
with every instance is the same confound: \emph{density}. Add edges and a system
becomes, all at once, denser, more frustrated, more closed, more cyclic,
\emph{and} more integrated. A monotone correlation between any structural
measure and any integration measure is therefore nearly content-free --- it can
be entirely a density artifact.

The sharper, falsifiable question is the partial one: \textbf{does a structural
invariant predict integration (or landscape ruggedness) after density is held
fixed?} If yes, the structure carries a signal that raw edge count does not. If
no, the folk theorem was density all along.

We answer this question on three deliberately unrelated substrates, each small
enough to enumerate \emph{exactly} --- no Monte-Carlo, no learned model, no
floating-point perplexity:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0.2em
  \item a $3{\times}3$ periodic Edwards--Anderson $\pm J$ Ising lattice
        ($N{=}9$ spins, $2^9 = 512$ configurations enumerated in full), where
        \emph{frustration} is the structural measure and energy-landscape
        \emph{ruggedness} (number of single-flip local minima) is the landscape
        measure;
  \item an $N{=}6$ directed category-graph (30 possible morphisms), where
        \emph{composition closure} is the structural measure and
        transitive-reachability density is the integration proxy;
  \item an $N{=}6$ undirected graph (15 possible edges), where the
        \emph{topological invariants} Betti-1 ($b_1 = E-V+C$) and Euler
        characteristic ($\chi = V-E$) are the structural measures and
        within-component reachability is the integration proxy.
\end{itemize}

Each substrate is run under a five-falsifier protocol that is \emph{frozen in
the harness source before the first run}; the post-hoc tuning count is zero. The
central falsifier on each substrate is the density-controlled one (F3): fix the
edge probability, split the instances at the median of the structural measure,
and ask whether the high-structure half out-integrates the low-structure half.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep0.3em
\item A single falsifiable formulation --- ``structure beyond density'' --- and
      its operationalization as a median-split, density-pinned F3 falsifier,
      applied identically to three substrates (\S\ref{sec:method}).
\item The positive result: F3 passes on all three substrates
      (\S\ref{sec:results}, Fig.~\ref{fig:f3spine}). Cycle topology and
      composition closure predict integration \emph{after} density is fixed;
      frustration amplifies ruggedness $1.6\times$.
\item Two pre-registered negatives, kept verbatim rather than deleted: the
      ferromagnet's $R{=}32$ (F1, H\_906) and the failure of random graphs to
      reach a closed category (F4, H\_907). Each negative falsifies a
      \emph{stronger} version of the folk theorem and is itself the finding.
\item A fully deterministic, $\$0$, CPU-only, bit-reproducible verification
      pipeline: closed-form \texttt{.hexa} harnesses whose raw stdout \emph{is}
      the verdict, persisted to \texttt{companion/verify-ledger.json}.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background}
\label{sec:bg}

\paragraph{Frustration and ruggedness.}
In an Edwards--Anderson Ising model~\citep{edwardsanderson1975} each bond carries
a fixed sign $J_{ij} \in \{+1,-1\}$ and the energy is
$E(s) = -\sum_{\langle ij\rangle} J_{ij}\, s_i s_j$ with $s_i\in\{\pm1\}$. A
plaquette (an elementary square of four bonds) is \emph{frustrated} when the
product of its bond signs is negative, so no spin assignment satisfies all four
bonds simultaneously~\citep{toulouse1977}. The frustration fraction $f$ is the
share of frustrated plaquettes. The folk claim is that frustration roughens the
landscape; we measure roughness as $R$, the number of \emph{single-flip local
minima} (configurations no single spin flip can lower).

\paragraph{Integrated information, operationalized.}
Integrated Information Theory~\citep{tononi2004,oizumi2014} posits a quantity
$\Phi$ measuring how much a system's whole exceeds its parts. Exact $\Phi$ is
super-exponential and intractable beyond toy sizes. Following the complexity
$\leftrightarrow\Phi$ tradition~\citep{lempelziv1976}, we use a tractable,
exactly-computable \emph{proxy}: reachability density. For a directed substrate,
$\Phi$ is the fraction of ordered node pairs connected by a path; for an
undirected substrate, it is the within-connected-component reachability density.
We are explicit that this is a proxy and claim nothing about full IIT-$\Phi$.

\paragraph{Composition closure.}
A directed graph is the underlying data of a \emph{category} only if it is closed
under composition: whenever $A\to B$ and $B\to C$ exist, $A\to C$ must
exist~\citep{maclane1971}. We measure closure as
$D = \#\text{present}(A\to C) / \#\text{composable}(A\to B \wedge B\to C)$ over
all distinct triples. $D{=}1$ is a genuine (composition-closed) category;
$D \ll 1$ is a mere graph.

\paragraph{Topological invariants of a 1-complex.}
For a graph viewed as a 1-dimensional simplicial complex, the Euler
characteristic is $\chi = V - E$ and the first Betti number (independent cycle
count) is $b_1 = E - V + C$, where $C$ is the number of connected
components~\citep{hatcher2002}. A tree has $b_1 = 0$ and $\chi = 1$; adding a
cycle raises $b_1$ and lowers $\chi$. These are exact integer invariants, ideal
for a closed-form test.

\paragraph{The confound this paper isolates.}
On every substrate, raising the edge/bond density raises both the structural
measure and the integration measure. The contribution is not the marginal
correlation (which we report only as a sanity control, F1/F2) but the
\emph{partial} one: the density-controlled F3.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\paragraph{Common protocol.}
All three harnesses share a skeleton: a deterministic linear-congruential
generator (LCG, $\text{state} \leftarrow (1103515245\,\text{state} + 12345)
\bmod 2^{31}$) seeds the bond/edge signs so that every run is bit-identical
across processes; the substrate is then enumerated \emph{exactly} (no sampling);
all measures are integer counts or exact rationals (no perplexity, no
floating-point training). The edge/bond probability $p$ is swept over
$\{0.2, 0.4, 0.6\}$ with 8 seeds each ($24$ instances), except the spin-glass,
which sweeps the negative-bond fraction $P_{\text{neg}} \in \{0, 0.25, 0.5\}$.
Each harness freezes five falsifiers \emph{in its source header before the first
run}; the verdict map is $5\,\text{PASS}\Rightarrow\tierGreen$ (5/5),
$4\Rightarrow\tierGreen$ (4/5), $3\Rightarrow\tierYellow$, $\le 2\Rightarrow
\tierRed$. The post-hoc tuning count across all three substrates is $0$: no
falsifier was edited after seeing a result.

\subsection{Substrate A --- spin-glass frustration $\to$ ruggedness (H\_906)}
$3{\times}3$ periodic EA $\pm J$ Ising lattice: $N{=}9$ spins, $18$ bonds
($9$ horizontal $+ 9$ vertical), $2^9 = 512$ configurations enumerated in full.
Energy $E(s) = -\sum_{\text{bond}} J\, s_a s_b$ (integer). Frustration
$f = (\#\text{frustrated plaquettes})/9$. Ruggedness $R = $ number of
single-flip local minima (configurations with no lower-energy neighbour among
their 9 single-flip neighbours). Pre-registered falsifiers:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0.15em
  \item \textbf{F1 FERRO-2MIN}: $P_{\text{neg}}{=}0$ (ferromagnet) $\Rightarrow$
        every instance has $f{=}0 \wedge R{=}2$ (only the two aligned ground
        states).
  \item \textbf{F2 FRUST-RUGGED}: $\bar R(P_{\text{neg}}{=}0.5) > \bar R(0)$.
  \item \textbf{F3 MONOTONE}: $\bar R$ non-decreasing across
        $P_{\text{neg}}\in\{0,0.25,0.5\}$.
  \item \textbf{F4 FRUST-PRESENT}: $\exists$ instance with $f>0$ at
        $P_{\text{neg}}{=}0.5$.
  \item \textbf{F5 CORR}: high-$f$ ($\ge 0.3$) group $\bar R >$ low-$f$
        ($<0.3$) group $\bar R$.
\end{itemize}
Here F5 plays the density-controlled role (F3 is the monotone control); the
strong, non-trivial claim is that $f$ separates $R$ within the swept range.

\subsection{Substrate B --- composition closure $\to$ integration (H\_907)}
$N{=}6$-object directed graph, $30$ possible morphisms (self-loops excluded,
identities implicit), edges seeded by the LCG. Closure
$D = \#\text{present}(A{\to}C) / \#\text{composable}(A{\to}B \wedge B{\to}C,\,
A\!\ne\!B\!\ne\!C)$, computed by exact triple enumeration. Integration
$\Phi = \#\{(A,B), A\!\ne\!B : A \leadsto B\} / 30$ via boolean transitive
closure (Floyd). Pre-registered falsifiers:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0.15em
  \item \textbf{F1 CLOSURE-RISES}: $\bar D(p{=}0.6) > \bar D(0.2)$ (control).
  \item \textbf{F2 INTEG-RISES}: $\bar\Phi(0.6) > \bar\Phi(0.2)$ (control).
  \item \textbf{F3 CONTROLLED}: at fixed $p{=}0.4$, the high-$D$ half has
        $\bar\Phi >$ the low-$D$ half --- \emph{the real, non-trivial claim}.
  \item \textbf{F4 CATEGORY-FORM}: $\exists\, p$ with $\bar D \ge 0.85$
        (near-closed = an emergent category).
  \item \textbf{F5 SPARSE-OPEN}: $\bar D(0.2) < 0.6$ (sparse graphs are far
        from closed).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Substrate C --- cycle topology $\to$ integration (H\_908)}
$N{=}6$ undirected graph, $15$ possible edges, LCG-seeded. Euler
$\chi = V - E$; Betti-1 $b_1 = E - V + C$ with $C$ the component count from a
BFS min-label relaxation fixed-point. Integration
$\Phi = \sum_{\text{comp}} |comp|(|comp|-1) / (N(N-1))$. The sweep adds one
deterministic path graph $P_6$ for an exact acyclic check. Pre-registered
falsifiers:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0.15em
  \item \textbf{F1 B1-RISES}: $\bar b_1(0.6) > \bar b_1(0.2)$ (control).
  \item \textbf{F2 PHI-RISES}: $\bar\Phi(0.6) > \bar\Phi(0.2)$ (control).
  \item \textbf{F3 CONTROLLED}: at fixed $p{=}0.4$, the high-$b_1$ half has
        $\bar\Phi >$ the low-$b_1$ half --- \emph{the real, non-trivial claim}.
  \item \textbf{F4 EULER-NEG}: $\chi$ anti-couples with $\Phi$
        ($\bar\chi(0.2) > \bar\chi(0.6)$ while $\bar\Phi(0.2) < \bar\Phi(0.6)$).
  \item \textbf{F5 TREE-EXACT}: the deterministic path $P_6$ gives
        $b_1 = 0 \wedge \chi = 1$ exactly.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Worked examples (one instance per substrate)}
\label{sec:worked}
To make the measures concrete and the enumeration auditable by hand, we trace a
single small instance on each substrate.

\paragraph{A --- one frustrated plaquette.}
Take the unit square with bonds $(s_1 s_2), (s_2 s_3), (s_3 s_4), (s_4 s_1)$ and
signs $J = (+,+,+,-)$. The plaquette product is
$(+)(+)(+)(-) = -1 < 0$, so the plaquette is frustrated: no assignment of the
four spins satisfies all four bonds at once (any choice leaves at least one bond
unsatisfied), which is exactly why frustration creates competing low-energy
patterns. Flip the all-up state $s=(+,+,+,+)$: three bonds are satisfied, the
$-$ bond is not, $E = -2$; every single flip either keeps $E$ or raises it for
some neighbour, so the local-minima count $R$ picks up structure a single
aligned ground state cannot have. Multiply this over the $3{\times}3$ periodic
lattice and $R$ climbs from $32$ (no frustrated plaquettes) toward $55$.

\paragraph{B --- one composable triple.}
With objects $\{A,B,C\}$ and morphisms $A\to B$ and $B\to C$ present, the triple
$(A,B,C)$ is \emph{composable}. Closure asks whether $A\to C$ is also present. If
it is, this triple contributes $1/1$ to $D$; if not, $0/1$. A graph that is dense
but ``open'' (many composable triples whose composite is missing) has high edge
count yet low $D$ --- which is precisely the regime F3 exploits: two graphs at
the same density can differ sharply in $D$, and the higher-$D$ one reaches more
of its objects ($\Phi$).

\paragraph{C --- one independent cycle.}
The triangle on $\{1,2,3\}$ with edges $\{12,23,31\}$ has $V{=}3$, $E{=}3$, one
component, so $b_1 = E - V + C = 3 - 3 + 1 = 1$ (one independent cycle) and
$\chi = V - E = 0$. Removing any one edge gives a path: $b_1$ drops to $0$ and
$\chi$ rises to $1$ --- the exact tree invariants checked by F5 on $P_6$. The
cycle is what lets all three vertices reach each other in both directions; the
path loses that, and $\Phi$ falls. This is the per-instance engine behind the
density-controlled F3 of Fig.~\ref{fig:f3spine}.

\paragraph{Negative 1 (F1, H\_906): frustration amplifies, it does not create.}
The pre-registered F1 predicted that an unfrustrated ferromagnet has exactly two
single-flip minima (the two aligned ground states). It is false: the ferromagnet
has $R = 32$. Metastable domain configurations exist with \emph{zero}
frustration. The correct statement is therefore weaker and more interesting than
the folk theorem --- frustration \emph{amplifies} ruggedness ($R: 32 \to 42 \to
55$ as $P_{\text{neg}}: 0 \to 0.25 \to 0.5$) rather than \emph{creating} it. We
keep F1 falsified rather than rewriting it post-hoc.

\paragraph{Negative 2 (F4, H\_907): random density does not make a category.}
The pre-registered F4 predicted that at some swept density the graph would be
near-closed ($\bar D \ge 0.85$), i.e. a category would \emph{emerge} from random
morphisms. It is false: even at $p{=}0.6$, $\bar D = 0.576$. Composition closure
is not a density phenomenon; it requires designed structure. This sharpens the
positive F3 result: closure \emph{predicts} integration beyond density (F3 PASS),
yet closure itself does \emph{not} arise from density (F4 fail). Both halves are
findings.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Why the same partial effect recurs across unrelated substrates.}
The three substrates share no physics: a frustrated magnet, a small category,
and an undirected graph have different state spaces, different measures, and
different folk theorems attached to them. Yet the same \emph{shape} of result
appears in all three --- a structural invariant that retains predictive power
once density is partialled out. We read this as evidence that the relevant
quantity in each case is not the \emph{amount} of connection (density) but the
\emph{arrangement} of it. Frustration is a sign-arrangement of bonds; closure is
a triple-arrangement of morphisms; Betti-1 is a cycle-arrangement of edges. Each
is a second-order property --- a constraint on how first-order density is wired
--- and it is the second-order property that survives the F3 control. A plausible
common mechanism is that arrangement, not count, determines how the state space
folds: frustration multiplies the number of locally-stable spin patterns, closure
and cycles multiply the number of mutually-reachable states. Density sets the
budget; arrangement spends it. We state this as an interpretation, not a verified
claim --- the verified content is strictly the three F3 verdicts.

\paragraph{The predict-but-do-not-emerge dissociation.}
The H\_907 substrate produces a pair of results that are more interesting
together than apart. F3 passes: composition closure predicts integration beyond
density. F4 fails: composition closure does not itself emerge from density. Read
jointly, this is a \emph{dissociation} --- a property can be a strong predictor
of integration while being something that random density never supplies. It is a
useful caution against a common slide in the ``complexity begets X'' literature:
showing that a structure correlates with (or even predicts) an outcome says
nothing about whether that structure will \emph{arise} on its own. For categories
specifically, it is a small closed-form echo of the fact that category-hood is a
designed constraint (Yoneda structure does not fall out of a random digraph),
consistent with the textbook view that composition closure is an axiom one
imposes, not a statistic one observes~\citep{maclane1971}.

\paragraph{Relation to the integration literature.}
Our $\Phi$ is a reachability proxy, deliberately weaker than IIT-$\Phi$
\citep{tononi2004,oizumi2014}. The value of the proxy here is precisely that it
is exactly computable, so the F3 control is exact rather than estimated. The
results should be read as a statement about \emph{this} proxy: among small
substrates at fixed density, cyclic and composition-closed arrangements reach
more of their own state space than treelike or open ones. Whether the same
partial effect survives under full IIT-$\Phi$ is the open question of
Table~\ref{tab:pipeline}, stage~\textcircled{\scriptsize 7}, and is the natural
next experiment.

\section{Limitations and honest caveats}
\label{sec:limits}

\begin{itemize}\itemsep0.2em
\item \textbf{Proxy, not full $\Phi$.} ``Integration'' is reachability density,
      not IIT-$\Phi$, which is intractable at any non-toy size
      (stage~\textcircled{\scriptsize 7}, Tab.~\ref{tab:pipeline}, stays
      \tierOrange). The claim is about the proxy.
\item \textbf{Small substrates.} $N{=}9$ (spin-glass) and $N{=}6$ (graphs) are
      chosen for \emph{exact} enumeration. The F3 effect is established within
      these sizes; scaling behaviour is not swept here.
\item \textbf{Density range.} Each F3 is controlled at a single density
      ($p{=}0.4$); we do not claim the partial effect at every density, only at
      the pre-registered control point.
\item \textbf{Median split, not regression.} F3 is a two-group comparison, not a
      partial-correlation coefficient. It answers ``does high-structure beat
      low-structure at fixed density?'' --- a directional, not an effect-size,
      claim.
\item \textbf{$n$ per half is small} ($n{=}4$ for B,C). The effect is exact for
      these instances but is not a population estimate with error bars.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Across three substrates that share no physics --- frustrated magnets, small
categories, and graph topology --- a structural invariant predicts a
landscape/integration property \emph{after density is held fixed}. The
density-controlled F3 falsifier passes on all three (Fig.~\ref{fig:f3spine}). The
two pre-registered negatives are not failures of the program but its sharpest
output: frustration amplifies rather than creates ruggedness, and closed
categories do not emerge from random density. The single most important
follow-up is to replace the reachability proxy with full IIT-$\Phi$ on the same
exactly-enumerable substrates --- the only stage in Table~\ref{tab:pipeline}
still tiered \tierOrange.
