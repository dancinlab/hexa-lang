QUESTION
  Does the OEIS catalogue-mirror pattern (bulk numeric dump -> hash-intersect
  against hexa-native precompute -> hexa verify re-confirm -> atlas fold)
  generalize to DLMF (NIST Digital Library of Mathematical Functions)?

TERMINAL VERDICT
  RED / FALSIFIED (closed-negative) -- the OEIS exact-integer hash-intersect
  pattern does NOT generalize to DLMF as-is. Two of three structural
  preconditions FAIL deterministically (no bulk numeric corpus; hexa lacks the
  classical special functions). The pattern's portability is the falsified
  property. A DIFFERENT mechanism (numeric sampling + tolerance match) would be
  required, and even that is blocked today by the missing special-fn library.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. WHY OEIS WORKED (the 3 preconditions of the pattern)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  P1 BULK CORPUS    OEIS ships stripped.gz (~14MB packed, ~77MB unpacked):
                    one machine-readable {Annnnnn -> integer tuple} per line.
                    O(N) single download, parseable with awk.
  P2 UNIFORM REPR   Every entry is an INTEGER TUPLE (first ~80 terms). A tuple
                    is directly HASHABLE -- exact-equality key. No tolerance,
                    no representation ambiguity.
  P3 HEXA COVERAGE  hexa has ~20 candidate number-theoretic fns whose first K
                    terms are an integer tuple of the same shape
                    (sigma/tau/phi/mu/sigma_k/aliquot/is_perfect/...). Match =
                    exact tuple-hash collision. O1 POC: 6/899 hits, ~seconds.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. DLMF STRUCTURE (evidence)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  WebFetch dlmf.nist.gov/about + /help (2026-05-25):
    - Content delivered PER-FORMULA on web pages; formulas in MathML (+ LaTeX
      source). Browse + search UI. NO bulk download advertised, NO public API,
      NO downloadable formula corpus, NO bulk numeric-table dump.
    - Two canonical forms only: the printed NIST Handbook (Cambridge UP) and
      the free per-page electronic version. Neither is a dataset.
  Known structure of DLMF content:
    - DLMF = SPECIAL FUNCTIONS + IDENTITIES: Bessel J_nu/Y_nu, Gamma(z),
      error function erf, Hermite/Legendre/Laguerre/Chebyshev polynomials,
      Airy Ai/Bi, Riemann zeta, hypergeometric pFq, elliptic integrals, ...
    - An "entry" is a SYMBOLIC IDENTITY (e.g. recurrence, integral rep,
      generating function) or a CONTINUOUS function f(x) over a real/complex
      domain -- NOT a fixed integer tuple.
    - The few numeric tables are illustrative sample points, not a uniform
      bulk corpus, and are not offered as a downloadable dataset.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. STRUCTURAL DIFFERENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  OEIS:  id  -> integer tuple          (uniform, finite, EXACT-HASHABLE)
  DLMF:  id  -> symbolic identity OR continuous f : R/C -> R/C
                                       (heterogeneous, NOT hashable on a tuple)
  Each precondition maps to a FAILURE:
    P1 BULK CORPUS    FAIL  no stripped.gz-equivalent; per-page MathML only.
    P2 UNIFORM REPR   FAIL  symbolic/continuous; an exact integer-tuple hash
                            key does not exist. Float sampling needs TOLERANCE
                            matching (epsilon), which is a different mechanism,
                            not the OEIS exact-hash.
    P3 HEXA COVERAGE  FAIL  see section 5 -- hexa verify --expr exposes ZERO
                            classical special functions.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THREE SUB-OPTIONS ASSESSED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  (A) NUMERIC-TABLE INTERSECT
      Sample f(x) at fixed x, hash/tolerance-match vs hexa special-fn values.
      Blocked by TWO independent failures:
        (A.1) NO BULK DLMF SOURCE -- the tables are not bulk-downloadable
              (section 2). The O(N) single-fetch step has no analogue.
        (A.2) HEXA HAS NO SPECIAL FNS -- section 5. There is nothing to
              intersect against. Even if DLMF data existed, hexa cannot
              recompute Bessel/Gamma(x)/erf/Hermite/... to confirm a hit.
        (A.3) MECHANISM MISMATCH -- exact integer hash -> float tolerance
              match. Different algorithm (epsilon ball), not a "reuse" of the
              OEIS hash-intersect; would need a new sampling harness + a
              tolerance policy + a domain-grid spec. Net: NOT viable today.
  (B) IDENTITY-CITATION (register DLMF formula IDs as 🟡 citations)
      REJECTED by OEIS-domain governance (OEIS/OEIS.md sec 2): "naive dump
      forbidden, verify-gated only" + claim_verify "never auto-🔵 / no LLM
      self-judge". A wall of 🟡 citation rows with no hexa recompute is exactly
      the "catalogue identity drift" the domain bans. Also fails paper_gate
      (🟡 is non-terminal). NOT viable under governance.
  (C) CLOSED-NEGATIVE (pattern does not generalize)
      ADOPTED. DLMF is structurally incompatible with the OEIS exact-hash
      mirror. A symbolic-identity catalogue needs a fundamentally different
      ingest (numeric sampling + tolerance, or symbolic-identity verification),
      AND a hexa special-function library that does not yet exist. This is the
      honest, publishable O6 result (paper_negative_ok).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. HEXA SPECIAL-FUNCTION AVAILABILITY (decisive for option A)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Source: `hexa verify rubric` -- the calc-fn + float-fn registry that backs
  `hexa verify --expr` (the only verify-gated recompute surface).
    INTEGER calc fns : sigma sigma_0 sigma_2 phi mu tau is_perfect aliquot
                       gamma0_index gamma0_cusps gamma0_genus isotropy_lcm
                       first_cusp_form_weight pair_threshold_factor
                       recomb_3body_density_power ; 2-op sigma_k jacobi
                       kronecker dim_cusp_forms ssh_winding ; 3-op tknn_chern
    FLOAT fns (libm) : welch_t_crit wilson_hilferty_p compound_coverage
                       cycles_to_target chsh_tsirelson hardy_bound
                       cdr_perfect_mitigation vqe_h2_fci_sto3g qfi_sql qfi_ghz
                       ... (RFC 045 quantum bounds) ... exp_release ldl_pct
                       beer_lambert nnt arr ln_hr_to_hr ... (pharmacokinetics)
    grep for DLMF classical special fns over the FULL registry:
       bessel | erf | hermite | legendre | airy | zeta | hypergeom |
       gamma(x) [continuous] | tgamma | lgamma | elliptic | digamma |
       chebyshev | laguerre | sin( | cos(  ->  ZERO MATCHES
    CAUTION: `gamma0_index` is the MODULAR Gamma_0(N) index (a number-theoretic
       INTEGER), NOT the continuous Gamma function Gamma(z). It is irrelevant to
       DLMF special-function tables.
  CONCLUSION: hexa verify exposes NO classical special function. The domain
  governance ("fold scope = hexa-verify-able only") therefore makes option A
  impossible regardless of DLMF data availability -- there is no verify path.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. RULED-OUT AXIS + (CONDITIONAL) FUTURE PATH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  RULED OUT (deterministic): "DLMF can reuse the OEIS catalogue-mirror pattern
  as-is." FALSE on two independent counts (no bulk numeric corpus P1; no hexa
  special-fn library P3). The pattern is OEIS-shaped: it requires an EXACT,
  BULK, HASHABLE integer corpus + matching hexa integer fns. DLMF is none of
  these.
  A DLMF mirror is conceivable ONLY via a NEW mechanism, NOT this pattern:
    (i)  build a hexa special-function library (Bessel/Gamma/erf/orthogonal
         polys via libm or series) and register them in `hexa verify --expr`;
    (ii) replace exact integer-tuple hashing with NUMERIC SAMPLING on a fixed
         x-grid + TOLERANCE match (epsilon, 🟢 NUMERICAL tier, not 🔵 exact);
    (iii) source DLMF values by per-formula scrape (no bulk dump) OR an
         independent reference (mpmath/Boost) -- a heavier, identity-by-identity
         ingest, not an O(N) single download.
  All three are substantial NEW work; none is a "same-pattern" reuse. The O6
  probe therefore closes NEGATIVE on generalization, with (i)-(iii) logged as
  the prerequisite stack should a future "DLMF" sister domain be opened.
