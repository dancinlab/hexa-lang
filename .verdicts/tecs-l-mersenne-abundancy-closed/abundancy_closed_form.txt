------------------------------------------------------------------
Statement
------------------------------------------------------------------
Let p be a prime such that M_p = 2^p - 1 is a Mersenne prime.
Let P = 2^(p-1) * M_p (the Euclid-Euler even perfect number).
Then sigma(P) = 2P, equivalently abundancy(P) = sigma(P)/P = 2.

------------------------------------------------------------------
Derivation (4 elementary steps)
------------------------------------------------------------------
[S1] sigma is multiplicative over coprime factors:
       gcd(a,b) = 1  ==>  sigma(a*b) = sigma(a) * sigma(b).
     This is the standard property of multiplicative arithmetic
     functions (Hardy and Wright, Theorem 273 / equivalent).

[S2] gcd(2^(p-1), M_p) = 1.
     2^(p-1) is a pure power of 2; M_p = 2^p - 1 is odd
     (since 2^p is even, 2^p - 1 is odd). Hence the only common
     divisor is 1.

[S3] sigma of a prime power: sigma(q^k) = (q^(k+1) - 1)/(q - 1).
     With q=2, k=p-1: sigma(2^(p-1)) = (2^p - 1)/(2 - 1) = 2^p - 1 = M_p.

[S4] sigma of a prime: sigma(r) = r + 1 for prime r.
     With r = M_p (prime by hypothesis):
       sigma(M_p) = M_p + 1 = (2^p - 1) + 1 = 2^p.

[Combine] By [S1] applied with a = 2^(p-1), b = M_p (coprime by [S2]):
       sigma(P) = sigma(2^(p-1)) * sigma(M_p)
                = M_p * 2^p                       (by [S3] and [S4])
                = (2^p - 1) * 2^p
                = 2 * (2^(p-1) * (2^p - 1))
                = 2 * 2^(p-1) * M_p
                = 2 * P.                                         QED

Equivalently, abundancy(P) = sigma(P) / P = 2, i.e. P is perfect.

Honest scope
------------------------------------------------------------------
- The derivation covers ONLY even perfect numbers in the Euclid-Euler
  form P = 2^(p-1) * M_p, M_p Mersenne prime.
- Euler (1747) proved the converse: every EVEN perfect number has
  this form. Combined with the derivation above, this fully classifies
  the even perfect numbers.
- ODD PERFECT NUMBERS: existence is OPEN. No odd perfect number has
  been found nor proven impossible. MR3 makes no claim about them.
  This open problem is tracked separately as TECS-L axis B MR7
  (🟠 INSUFFICIENT/DEFERRED — honest fence).
