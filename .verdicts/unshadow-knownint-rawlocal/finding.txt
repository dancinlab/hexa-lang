FINDING (🔴 FALSIFIED — closed-negative, paper_negative_ok):
  clang -O2 ALREADY eliminates the inline `.i` box. SROA/mem2reg scalarizes
  the single-field {.tag,.i} compound literal: .tag is dead (never read in the
  loop), .i is promoted to register x8. The emitted hot loop is already
  `add x8,x8,#1`, byte-identical to the hand-written raw int64 local.

  Therefore the raw-int64-local lowering recovers ZERO additional Δ over the
  inline `.i` slice. The box surface the milestone targeted does NOT survive
  -O2 in the first place — there is nothing left to remove.

RULED-OUT AXIS:
  "Remove the surviving box compound literal to reach d_ideal" is a DEAD axis
  for a single-int-field accumulator under clang -O2. The optimizer's SROA is
  the actual box-eliminator; the inline `.i` slice (PR #2202) was already
  sufficient to hit the register-pack ceiling. The prior bench's d_ideal=0.08s
  vs f_inline=1.00s "12.5× gap" was a measurement artifact (different n /
  noise) — f_inline IS d_ideal at the asm level.

  Corollary: source-level box-stripping only matters where SROA CANNOT fire —
  i.e. multi-field / escaping / aliased HexaVals that cross the runtime.o ABI
  wall (the e_boxed 1.49x tax). That is the only remaining real lever.
