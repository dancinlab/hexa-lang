EUCLID-EULER CORRESPONDENCE — synthesis artifact (TECS-L axis B MR1)

THEOREM (Euclid-Euler).
  An even positive integer n is perfect (sigma(n) = 2n) if and only if
  n has the form
        n = 2^(p-1) * M_p,    where M_p = 2^p - 1 is a Mersenne prime.

  Euclid (Elements IX.36, c. 300 BCE): sufficiency — if 2^p - 1 is prime,
    then 2^(p-1) * (2^p - 1) is perfect.
  Euler (posth. 1849, "De numeris amicabilibus"): necessity — every EVEN
    perfect number has this form.

  Whether any ODD perfect number exists is OPEN (deferred — TECS-L MR7).
  None below 10^1500 (Ochem-Rao 2012). Out of scope for this artifact.
