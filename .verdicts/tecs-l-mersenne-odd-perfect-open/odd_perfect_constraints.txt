ODD PERFECT NUMBER — open problem · known lower-bound table (TECS-L axis B MR7)
================================================================================

THE OPEN QUESTION.
  Does any ODD perfect number exist?
  Equivalently: does there exist an odd positive integer n with sigma(n) = 2n?
  Status: OPEN.  No proof of existence; no proof of non-existence.

  Complement to Euclid-Euler (TECS-L MR1):
    EVEN perfect numbers <=>  n = 2^(p-1) * M_p, M_p = 2^p - 1 a Mersenne prime
                              (Euclid IX.36 sufficiency + Euler 1849 necessity).
  No analogous characterization is known for the odd case. MR7 documents the
  modern lower bounds + structural constraints WITHOUT claiming closure.

  TECS-L g5 verdict tier  =  ORANGE INSUFFICIENT/DEFERRED.
  Reason: the known bounds are LOWER bounds (search lower bounds and structural
  necessary conditions) — they do NOT constitute a proof of non-existence.
  No g5 closed-form path settles existence at this time.

================================================================================

  --------------------------------------------------  ---------------------  ----------------------
  Magnitude lower bound                               n > 10^1500            Ochem-Rao 2012
  Distinct prime factor count   omega(n)              omega(n) >= 9          Nielsen 2015

  Largest prime factor          P(n)                  P(n) >= 10^8           Goto-Ohno 2008
  Second-largest prime factor                         >= 10^4                Iannucci 1999

  Total prime factor count      Omega(n)              Omega(n) >= 101        Ochem-Rao 2014
  Euler form (structure)                              n = p^a * q_1^(2 b_1)  Euler 1849
                                                          * ... * q_k^(2 b_k)
                                                      with p == a == 1 (mod 4)
                                                      and p prime, p NOT in {q_i}

  Each row is a NECESSARY CONDITION: any odd perfect n (if one exists) must
  satisfy it. None of them, jointly or individually, rules out existence.


EULER FORM (1849) — structural constraint.
================================================================================
  Every odd perfect number n (if any) is of the form
        n = p^a * q_1^(2 b_1) * q_2^(2 b_2) * ... * q_k^(2 b_k),
  where
    - p, q_1, ..., q_k are distinct odd primes,
    - p == 1 (mod 4)         (the "special" prime),
    - a == 1 (mod 4),
    - b_i >= 1 for all i,
    - the q_i are pairwise distinct and distinct from p.

  Equivalently: in the prime factorisation of n, exactly ONE prime power
  has an exponent congruent to 1 mod 4 (the "Euler prime" p^a), and every
  other prime power has an EVEN exponent.

  This is the deepest unconditional structural fact known.  It does not
  bound the omega / Omega / magnitude axes on its own — those are handled
  by the rows of the table above.
